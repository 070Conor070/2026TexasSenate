How polls are weighted

Each poll receives a composite weight combining three factors from Nate Silver's FiveThirtyEight methodology:

  • Grade multiplier (50%) — Silver Bulletin letter grade. A+ = 4.00×, D- = 0.25×. Replaces the old simple A/B/C/D system with the full 13-tier scale.
  • Recency decay (35%) — Exponential. This week = 100%. 30 days old ≈ 50%. 60 days ≈ 25%. Polls over 90 days are excluded unless fewer than 3 remain.
  • Sample size (15%) — Log-scaled. 1,000-voter poll ≈ 2× weight of 250-voter poll. Per Silver's 2024 update, sample size is de-emphasized since modern sampling deviates from classical assumptions.

A house-effect correction of +2.5R is applied to the final average, accounting for Texas Democrats' persistent polling overperformance since 2018 (avg ~+3–5pt D overshoot).

Swingometer methodology

Starts from the selected historical baseline's D% and R%, then applies slider shifts weighted by each demographic's share of the Texas electorate:

  • Latino shift: 40% TX weight — Rio Grande Valley, El Paso, SA
  • Suburban college: 20% TX weight — Collin, Fort Bend, Williamson
  • Rural mobilization: 25% TX weight — inverse (R benefits)
  • Independent lean: 15% TX weight — cross-pressured exurban
  • GOP crossover: direct D addition — models runoff defectors. TPOR found ~24% of Cornyn voters say they'd back Talarico vs Paxton.

County map applies each swing at county level, scaled by county type (urban/suburban/rural/Latino).

Context & caveats

  • No Democrat has won a Texas statewide race since 1994.
  • Recent D polling overshoot: ~+3–5pt avg vs actual results.
  • Cook Political Report: "Likely Republican."
  • GOP nominee TBD — May 26 runoff: Cornyn vs Paxton.
  • TPOR directed by Democratic strategist Luke Warford — treat with elevated skepticism despite nonpartisan branding (grade B).
  • UT/Texas Politics Project surveys RVs not LVs — historically inflates D numbers by 2–4pt.
  • Emerson grade: A (SB Jan 2026). Historically solid on TX (called Cruz +8 in 2018).
  • Quinnipiac: A+ — largest sample, live-caller, highest default weight.
