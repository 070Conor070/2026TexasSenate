How polls are weighted

This aggregator assigns each poll a composite weight based on three factors:

  • Pollster grade (40%): Based on historical accuracy and methodology. A+ polls count roughly 4x more than D-rated polls.
  • Recency (40%): Exponential decay — polls from this week count 100%, 30-day-old polls ~50%, 60+ days ~25%.
  • Sample size (20%): Log-scaled. A 1,000-person poll gets ~2× the weight of a 250-person poll.

The final weighted average excludes polls older than 90 days unless fewer than 3 polls exist.

Swingometer methodology

The swingometer uses each historical baseline's D and R percentages, then applies sliders as additive shifts, weighted by demographic share of the Texas electorate:

  • Latino shift: ~40% TX weight, heavily impacts Rio Grande Valley
  • Suburban college: ~20% TX weight, strongest in Dallas/Houston suburbs
  • Rural mobilization: ~25% TX weight, inverse (R benefits from higher rural turnout)
  • Independent lean: ~15% TX weight, cross-pressured voters
  • GOP crossover: direct addition to D vote share, models Cornyn/Paxton primary fallout

Context & caveats

No Democrat has won a Texas statewide race since 1994. Historical polling has systematically overestimated Democratic performance.

Cook Political Report rates this race "Likely Republican." This aggregator applies a modest house effect correction of +2.5R to account for Texas Democrats' historical polling overperformance.

The Republican nominee is still TBD — a May 26 runoff between Cornyn and Paxton will determine who faces Talarico in November.
